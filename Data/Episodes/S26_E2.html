"The Wreck of the Relationship" is the 2nd episode of the 26th season of the animated series The Simpsons , and the 554th episode of the series overall. It aired on October 5, 2014, in the United States, on the Fox network. The episode sees Marge send Homer and Bart onto a ship where their father-son conflict can be resolved, while she is left to manage her husband's fantasy football team.

Plot [edit]

When Bart disrespects his father Homer's authority, Homer tries to parent Bart. Bart later refuses to eat one piece of broccoli at dinner, so Homer decides to sit at the table until Bart eats his broccoli, which lasts two days with each of them getting a temptation from their friends. When they eventually start to fight, Marge sends the two to a "Relation Ship" with Captain Bowditch (Nick Offerman), where they can solve their relationship problems. Bart excels on the ship and is made second-in-command, but Homer detests it. The captain, recovering from alcoholism, sees Homer drinking a flask of rum and joins in, becoming drunk. A storm begins, and Bart is in charge of the ship due to the captain's intoxication; Homer refuses to accept Bart's authority until he finally eats the broccoli. The crew steer the boat to safety. [1]

Meanwhile, as Homer could not move from the table when Bart was refusing to eat broccoli, Marge had to draft Homer's fantasy football team ("Somewhere Over the Dwayne Bowe") despite knowing nothing of the sport. Initially put off by the trash talking from Homer's friends, she and Lisa study football in detail and end up winning the Fantasy League.

Production [edit]

Nick Offerman voiced the captain.

In an interview with Entertainment Weekly , Parks and Recreation actor Nick Offerman revealed that he had been recommended for the voice of Captain Bowditch by Mike Scully, a writer on both The Simpsons and Parks and Recreation. Offerman never before thought that he would play a role on The Simpsons as "They’re writing parts for Paul McCartney", and was delighted to be cast as a sailor due to his interest in naval literature. [1]

Reception [edit]

The episode received an audience of 4.27 million, the highest on Fox that night. [2]

Dennis Perkins of The A.V. Club gave the episode a B-, calling the series' habit of relying on family-based plots "both an infinite resource and a curse". He wrote that "Bowditch doesn’t have much of a personality, and the antics on the ship are benignly ordinary...Apu, Ned, Cletus, and some assorted sons are along for the ride, but don’t contribute much, either." However, he praised the sub-plot due to Marge's reactions to a male-dominated game. [3]
